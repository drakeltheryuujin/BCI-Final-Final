Mitigating Climate Change Through Coastal Ecosystem Management

Coastal ecosystems are some of the most productive on Earth. They support many ecosystem services and we have recently come to recognize another integral service - sequestering and storing carbon from the atmosphere and oceans. The carbon storage potential of coastal ecosystems, also known as "blue carbon", greatly helps to mitigate global climate change.The International Blue Carbon Initiative is a coordinated, global program focused on mitigating climate change through the conservation and restoration of coastal and marine ecosystems.

What is blue carbon

Blue carbon is the carbon stored in coastal and marine ecosystems. The natural coastal ecosystems of seagrasses, tidal marshes, and mangroves sequester and store large quantities of blue carbon in both the plants and the sediment below them.

Learn more about the science

Where is it?

The Blue Carbon Initiative focuses on mangroves, salt marshes and seagrasses, which are found on every continent except Antarctica.

Why is it important?

  • Blue carbon ecosystems sequester and store carbon
  • When degraded or destroyed, these ecosystems emit the carbon they have stored for centuries back into the atmosphere and oceans and become sources of greenhouse gases
  • These ecosystems also contribute to healthy water quality, support fisheries, and buffer coasts against floods and storms
About

What is being done?

The Blue Carbon Initiative works to protect and restore coastal ecosystems for their role in reducing impacts of global climate change.
